Norfolk Solar FAQs

Are solar panels actually worth it in Norfolk?

Absolutely. With Norfolk's decent 4.0 peak sun hours, 0% VAT until March 2027, and typical ¬£1,950/year savings, most systems pay for themselves in 6-8 years then give you 15+ years of virtually free electricity. Commercial systems often pay back in just 3-5 years.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes, they work brilliantly. Panels generate electricity from daylight, not just direct sunshine. Even on Norfolk's cloudiest days, you'll still get 20-30% of peak output. It's the yearly performance that matters, not daily ups and downs.

What's this about selling electricity back to the grid?

When your panels produce more than you're using, the excess automatically goes to the grid and you get paid through the Smart Export Guarantee (SEG). Rates vary but typically 4-15p per kWh ‚Äì that's an extra ¬£200-600/year in your pocket.

Do I need a battery with my solar panels?

Not essential, but batteries store excess solar for evening use, increasing savings by 20-40%. Without a battery, you use solar during the day and grid electricity at night. Our quote system shows costs and benefits of both options.

Can I charge my electric car with solar?

Yes! Solar + EV charging is highly effective in Norfolk. Charge your car with free solar electricity during the day. A typical home system can provide 8,000-12,000 miles of free EV driving per year.

Is there funding or grants available?

Solar has 0% VAT until March 2027 (saving ¬£800-1,600). Some local councils offer green home grants. Low-cost finance is available through established providers. Commercial premises may access additional business grants.

How long do solar panels last and do they need maintenance?

Panels last 25+ years with minimal maintenance - just occasional cleaning and annual checks. Inverters may need replacement after 10-15 years. Most systems come with 20-25 year performance warranties.

What if my roof isn't suitable for solar panels?

Our AI analysis checks roof suitability instantly - age, material, direction, and shading. Most Norfolk roofs work well. South-facing is ideal, but east/west roofs still deliver good returns. Even north-facing commercial roofs can be viable for large systems.
