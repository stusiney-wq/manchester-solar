## 1. Project Summary
The Solar Domain Empire is a network of premium .solar websites — led by the hub FindSolar.solar — built to dominate local search for solar panel installations ethically and sustainably.

Each city or region gets its own .solar site (e.g., Manchester.solar, Cambridge.solar) with:

Exclusive installer partner.

Dual-audience (residential & commercial) content.

GDPR-compliant, no-spam lead forms.

Geo-specific stats, grants, and council links.

## 2. Purpose
Connect homeowners & businesses with one vetted, MCS-certified installer per city.

Deliver exclusive, high-quality leads with zero competition or reselling.

Provide a stress-free, scalable business model for Stuart, the owner.

## 3. Target Audiences
Residential: homeowners, landlords, housing associations.

Commercial: offices, warehouses, retail, agriculture, schools, community sites.

## 4. USPs
Exclusive, high-intent leads.

GDPR-first — no spam, no resold data.

Local-first content with real stats, grants, and planning links.

Premium brand & UX (Octopus Energy × Tesla Solar feel).

## **Section 22 — SGE & AI Overview Optimization**  
*(Added 2025-08-11)*  

The **Search Generative Experience (SGE)** and other AI-powered search layers (Bing Copilot, Perplexity, ChatGPT Search) are shifting how users discover and consume content. These AI systems summarise and synthesise trusted sources into instant answers. Our `.solar` sites must be optimised to be the **primary quoted source** in these overviews.

### **22.1 SGE-Optimised Content Rules**
- **40–60 word authoritative summaries** at the start of each section and FAQ answer.  
- **Bold key terms** and figures for scanability (e.g., **3.8 peak sun hours**, **0% VAT until 2027**).  
- **Direct answers first**, then context (inverted pyramid style).  
- **Local references** (city name, landmarks, council) in early sentences.  
- **Source-worthy phrasing** — neutral, factual, trustworthy.

### **22.2 Schema for SGE**
- Ensure **FAQPage**, **LocalBusiness**, **Service**, and **WebPage** schema are valid on every page.  
- Mark up stats, benefits, and grants with **FAQ** and **HowTo** schema where relevant.  
- Keep **JSON-LD structured data** clean and up to date.

### **22.3 Link Earning for AI Confidence**
- Link to **credible local sources** (e.g., council sites, GOV.UK) to boost trust.  
- Use **descriptive anchor text** ("Manchester City Council solar planning guidance") instead of "click here."  
- Reference sources in content: "According to Manchester City Council…"

### **22.4 Formatting for AI Summaries**
- Use **short bullet lists** for steps, benefits, or stats — these are lifted cleanly into SGE answer cards.  
- Include **tables** for comparisons (e.g., savings by system size).  
- Ensure headings are **question-focused** where possible ("Do solar panels work in Manchester?").
- Use **HowTo schema** on /quote-process/ (5-step ordered list) for AI answer cards.

### **22.5 Voice & Conversational Search**
- Phrase key headings as **natural questions** users might speak.  
- Keep answers **plain-language, non-technical**.  
- Include **local context** in answers (weather, architecture, grid rates).

### **22.6 Image & Media for SGE**
- **Geo-tag images** and use descriptive alt text mentioning the city and installation type.  
- Include at least one **hero image** suitable for rich cards and previews.  
- Maintain **<150KB** image sizes for speed.

### **22.7 Performance & Engagement Signals**
- Maintain Core Web Vitals targets (Section 18.7).  
- Reduce bounce rate with **clear CTAs** near top and mid-page.  
- Use **sticky "Get a Quote" buttons** on mobile.

### **22.8 SGE Tracking & Iteration**
- Monitor AI Overview presence in Search Console (when rolled out).  
- Track which sections/phrases are being quoted and **refine them for clarity and authority**.  
- A/B test summary tone and structure for higher inclusion rates.
